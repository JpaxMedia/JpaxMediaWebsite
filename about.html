Story

How I got here.

01

From creative work to systems work

I came up through video, content, and brand work. The deeper I got, the more I saw the same pattern: businesses didn’t need more creative output — they needed the layer underneath that organized the output, captured the leads, and reported on what was working.

02

JPAX started as the answer

I built JPAX so I could deliver the website, the content rhythm, the dashboard, and the workflow as one operating system — not five different vendors. Founder-led so the buyer talks directly to the person doing the work.

03

Built on real proof

The internal cockpit I use to run JPAX is Jupiter. The vertical for trades is TradeOS. The creator layer is Elara. Everything you see on this site was built through Jupiter first, then shipped as a product or a client engagement.

Operating Principles

How I work.

i.

You own everything.

Your site, your content system, your data, your dashboards. JPAX doesn’t hold infrastructure hostage. If we stop working together, you walk away with the keys.

ii.

Founder-led, end to end.

The person reading your project brief is the person designing, building, and shipping it. No SDRs, no junior teams, no handoff loss.

iii.

Systems over campaigns.

Marketing campaigns burn. Operating systems compound. I’ll always push toward the layer that keeps producing after the launch.

iv.

AI as a tool, not a personality.

I use AI heavily inside the work — drafting, summarizing, automating. But I don’t hand creative judgment to a model. The voice, the strategy, the relationship stay human.
