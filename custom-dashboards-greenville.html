FAQ

Custom dashboard questions, answered.

What is a custom business dashboard?

A custom business dashboard pulls the numbers that actually run your business into one screen: leads, jobs, revenue, content, or whatever matters most. Unlike a generic report, it is built around the specific decisions you make, so you can see what is happening and act without digging through three different tools.

How is a custom dashboard different from a CRM report or a tool like Looker?

Off-the-shelf reports show you what the tool decided to measure, scattered across each platform. A custom dashboard pulls from all of those sources into one view shaped around your business and your decisions. JPAX builds the dashboard you own, not another login that shows charts nobody uses.

What should a dashboard actually show?

Only what supports a decision. A good dashboard answers questions like: are we getting enough leads, are they being followed up, what is driving revenue, and where is work stuck. JPAX deliberately leaves out vanity metrics that look impressive but change nothing about what you do next.

Where does the data come from?

From the tools you already use: your website and forms, inbox, spreadsheets, and existing software. JPAX connects those sources so the dashboard updates automatically instead of relying on someone copying numbers into a spreadsheet every week.

How much does a custom dashboard cost in Greenville?

A focused dashboard or reporting build starts at $5,000 as a Workflow Sprint. Larger operating dashboards that tie into automation are scoped by system. Cost depends on how many data sources connect and how much is custom-built.

Do I own the dashboard?

Yes. JPAX builds owned systems. The dashboard, the connections, and the reporting belong to your business rather than being locked inside a platform you rent indefinitely.
