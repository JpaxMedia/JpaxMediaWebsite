FAQ

Service business web design questions, answered.

What makes a good website for a service business?

A service-business website earns trust fast and makes it easy to take the next step. That means clear proof you do good work, real photos, service areas, honest pricing signals, and an obvious way to call or request a quote on a phone. Looking good is not enough. It has to convert a stranger into a booked job.

Do you build websites for contractors and home-service businesses?

Yes. Contractors, trades, and home-service teams are JPAX's core focus: paving, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, cleaning, and similar local operators. JPAX has built real systems for service businesses, including the Southern Pavers project.

Will my website show up on Google for local searches?

JPAX builds sites on a solid technical SEO foundation: clean structure, fast load times, local and service schema, and pages aimed at the searches your customers actually type. Ranking also depends on your Google Business Profile and reviews, and JPAX can guide that local setup alongside the website.

Can customers book or request a quote from the site?

Yes. Every JPAX service-business site is built around clear lead capture: quote requests, click-to-call, and intake that routes leads into a follow-up rhythm so they do not get lost. The website is the front door to a real lead flow, not a dead end.

How much does a service business website cost?

Local service-business websites start at $3,500 for a Local Website Foundation and scale to $5,000 and up for larger teams and more pages or integrations. Pricing depends on scope and whether the site connects to follow-up and reporting. You own what gets built.

I already have a website. Can you rebuild it?

Yes. JPAX regularly replaces dated or underperforming sites with faster, trust-first websites that actually generate leads. JPAX keeps what works, fixes what does not, and makes sure you own the result instead of renting it from a template platform.
