Why JPax is now an AI agent platform for creator-led businesses

I spent the last year retooling JPax. Today I'm locking the direction. This is the post I'll point everyone to.

If you've watched JPax over the last twelve months, you've watched a company quietly disassemble itself and rebuild from the inside. The work was always real — six production systems shipped, a custom agency platform running every client, an AI agent live in production. The audience was always clear-eyed. The direction was the part I kept refining.

It's locked now. JPax is the AI agent team for creator-led businesses. Everything else is downstream of that decision.

The problem I kept seeing

I'm a creator. I've been one for a long time. @thebatsreacts and @heroes_on_screen are mine — entertainment brands I built from zero on TikTok. I know what it actually takes to keep two creator brands running while also operating a business.

The pattern I kept watching, in myself and in every creator-led business around me, is the same: the business of being a creator eats the creating. Once income gets real, the operational load explodes — DMs, brand deals, posting cadence, cross-platform, edits, admin. You stop being a creator and start being a small business owner who used to make content.

The tools market saw that and answered it the way the tools market always answers things: with more tools. Schedulers, CRMs, dashboards, AI writers, AI editors. Three hundred dollars a month spread across nine platforms, and the bottleneck moved one click downstream.

Tools don't solve the operational load. They redistribute it.

The bottleneck isn't tools. The bottleneck is that creator-led businesses need a team they can't afford and don't want to manage.

The insight

Agents are different. A scheduler is a tool. A CRM is a tool. An agent is something else — a unit of capability that can take action, hold context, and operate inside a workflow without you driving it. One agent is interesting. A stack of agents wired together is an operating system. Once you have one, you don't need a team. You have a team.

That's the bet underneath JPax now. Everything I've built over the last year — Jupiter, Nova, the four shipped content engines, the custom apps — was an architecture experiment. I was learning whether the agent-stack pattern actually works in production, or whether it falls apart the moment real money depends on it.

The answer is that it works. Nova has been running live admin actions inside Jupiter for months. The Southern Pavers content engines have been publishing weekly on auto-pilot for a year. The architecture is real. The remaining question was who to point it at.

What JPax is now

JPax is building Elara — the AI agent team for creator-led businesses. Seven core agents, five modular extensions, and the operating system the creator economy was missing.

The seven agents handle the work creators don't want to do but can't escape:

  • Ideation — pulls research, trends, and content angles tuned to your niche
  • Writing — drafts in your voice (trained on your existing content)
  • Production — generates thumbnails, episode art, posting copy
  • Posting — schedules and publishes across platforms
  • Community — drafts replies to DMs and comments for your approval
  • Monetization — manages the brand-deal pipeline and follow-ups
  • Growth — analytics, A/B reads, and what to do next

The modular extensions activate on top of the core stack for creators who run courses, coaching, paid newsletters, product brands, or community networks. Mix what fits. Skip what doesn't.

And the relationship JPax sells around Elara isn't software. It's an operating partnership. JPax doesn't hand you tools and walk away. JPax runs the system on your behalf. You stay creative. We run the rest.

The proof

I don't expect anyone to take this seriously without a track record underneath it. So here's what's already shipped before the pivot:

  • Jupiter — JPax's own custom agency platform. Every client is onboarded, briefed, and tracked inside it. Built on the same Supabase + agent architecture Elara runs on.
  • Nova — the AI agent embedded inside Jupiter, running real admin actions in production daily. Per-org configurable, RLS-secured, scoped permissions. The pattern works.
  • 4 content engines publishing weekly on auto-pilot for trades and creator clients. The Southern Pavers engine alone has produced a year of zero-effort SEO content.
  • 6+ shipped products across creator, trades, and hospitality categories. TradeOS in production. The original CreatorOS architecture battle-tested.

Elara isn't a v1. It's the seventh iteration of an architecture I've been refining inside production work for two years.

The plan

The next 90 days are a focused sprint. I'm deploying Elara on my own creator accounts — @heroes_on_screen and @thebatsreacts — before I sell it to anyone else.

The reason is simple: I won't ship a system to creators that I haven't first proven on my own brands. The creator economy doesn't need another agency selling a system the founder doesn't use. The case study is going to be me, in public, every week, until the numbers are real enough that the bet stops feeling like a bet.

Every week of the sprint, I'll post a build log here. What got shipped. What broke. What the numbers are. No buzzwords, no agency fluff, no "transformation stories." Just the work, as it happens.
