FAQ

Greenville business automation questions, answered.

What is business automation?

Business automation is using software and AI to handle repetitive work that currently eats your time: capturing leads, sending follow-ups, moving information between tools, and pulling together reports. Done well, it does not replace your judgment. It removes the manual steps so the business runs without depending on someone remembering to do them.

What can you automate in a small business?

Common starting points are lead intake from your website and forms, follow-up sequences so no lead goes cold, internal handoffs and reminders, content and reporting rhythm, and dashboards that pull numbers together automatically. JPAX scopes automation around the specific points where your business loses time or loses leads.

Will AI make changes to my business data automatically?

No. JPAX builds AI workflows with human approval before anything touches real business data. AI can draft follow-ups, summarize, and prepare actions, but a person confirms before money, records, or customer-facing messages change. You stay in control.

Do I need new software, or does this work with my current tools?

Usually it works with what you already have. JPAX connects your website, inbox, forms, and existing tools rather than forcing a rip-and-replace. Where a custom dashboard or operating tool makes more sense than another subscription, JPAX can build one you own.

How much does business automation cost in Greenville?

Focused automation builds start at $5,000 for a Workflow Sprint covering a specific dashboard, intake flow, or automation. Larger operating-layer work is scoped by system. Pricing depends on how many tools connect and how much is custom-built versus configured.

How is this different from just buying a CRM?

A CRM is a tool you still have to operate. JPAX builds the system around the tool, so intake, follow-up, content, and reporting actually connect and run. The goal is an operating layer that fits how your business works, not another login that sits unused.
